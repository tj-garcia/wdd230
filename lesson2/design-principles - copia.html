White Space

Google.

www.google.com

Google uses white space (or “negative space”) as an empty space between, or, around elements of its page. Here it emphasizes certain elements such as input box to search information or the right corner icons for mail or others google tools. In this way also improve readability, divide elements without using visible dividers and create a feeling of elegance.

Visual Hierarchy

The Church of Jesus Chris of Latter-Day Saints>

www.churchofjesuschrist.org

This site uses rank design elements in the order it wants its users to view them. It uses scale, balance and, more to place and help the most important elements stand out like transmissions, week class, current announcements, and so on.

PARC; Contrast

Tripadvisor

www.tripadvisor.com

The design of Netflix's landing page minimizes the user's travel distance with a mouse in selecting an option for there's only one option at the beginning. It reduces time and increases productivity. Netflix doesn't give much option. It's either you sign up or hit the get started button. Just like the Fitt's law itself, the longer the user hit either of the button, the longer he is to explore the site.
